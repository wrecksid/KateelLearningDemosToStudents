# Attribution & Mandatory Requirements

This page summarises the mandatory obligations for anyone using material from this repository.
The full policy lives in
[ATTRIBUTION.md](https://github.com/VinayaSharada/KateelLearningDemosToStudents/blob/main/ATTRIBUTION.md).

---

## Three Mandatory Actions

### ① Credit in Every Presentation

Include the following statement on **every** slide deck, handout, lab sheet, or published
resource that uses or derives from this material:

> *"Demos and course material adapted from the* ***KateelLearningDemosToStudents*** *repository
> by Professor Vinaya Sathyanarayana (GitHub: VinayaSharada).*
> *https://github.com/VinayaSharada/KateelLearningDemosToStudents"*

This applies to:
- Presentation slides (title or acknowledgements slide)
- Lecture notes and printed handouts
- Lab sheets and assignment briefs
- Course-management-system pages and syllabi
- Recorded video descriptions and transcripts
- Published reports, theses, or conference papers

---

### ② Star the Repository ⭐

[**Star the repo here →**](https://github.com/VinayaSharada/KateelLearningDemosToStudents/stargazers)

This must be done before or at the point of first use.

---

### ③ Send a Mandatory Usage Notification 📧

Email **vinallcontact@gmail.com** with:

- **Subject:** `[KateelLearningDemos] Usage Notification — <Your Name / Institution>`
- **Body:**
  - Your name and role (faculty / student / researcher / practitioner / other)
  - Your institution or organisation
  - The course name, event, or project in which the material is used
  - Approximate start date
  - A link to your course page, slides, or project (if public)

Send this notification the **first time** you use the material, and again each time you
begin a new course, cohort, or project.

---

## After Notifying

Once you've sent your email and starred the repo, please self-register in the
**[Usage Registry](Usage-Registry)** — it takes under a minute and helps the community
see the global reach of this work.

---

## Why Are These Mandatory?

These requirements exist so that:

- Students and attendees know where to find the original material and future updates
- The author can track educational impact and reach out with improvements
- The open-source community of educators building on this work is acknowledged
- Academic integrity norms around derivative educational resources are respected
