🎯 What PM decision is being made? Setting the confidence threshold that balances automation rate, review burden, and business cost. This tradeoff is central to any AI-assisted product decision.
📊 What data is assumed? Scenario curves are representative but synthetic. Real ROC curves are model- and dataset-specific, and can shift with distribution changes (seasonality, adversarial drift).
⚠️ What can go wrong if you trust this tool too much? Real review queue costs include agent time, tooling, and training overhead. This model ignores queue backlog effects, reviewer fatigue, and SLA penalties for delayed decisions.
💡 What would a strong PM override here? A PM might accept higher false-negative cost short-term to reduce review burden during team scaling. Or they might set asymmetric cost weights after consulting Legal on regulatory exposure.
